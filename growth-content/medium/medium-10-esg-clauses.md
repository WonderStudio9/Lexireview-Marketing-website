# ESG Clauses in Indian Commercial Contracts: A Drafting Guide

> **Import this article using Medium's import tool (medium.com/p/import) with canonical URL: https://lexireview.in/blog/esg-clauses-indian-commercial-contracts-drafting**

SEBI now requires the top 150 listed companies to file BRSR reports. But ESG obligations cascade into every vendor agreement, procurement contract, and JV deed. If your supply chain partner violates environmental norms, it lands on your BRSR scorecard.

ESG compliance is no longer a corporate communications exercise. From FY 2024-25, SEBI requires the top 250 listed entities to obtain BRSR Core metrics from their value chain partners. This means ESG obligations are now contractual obligations --- and if your contracts do not reflect this reality, your compliance framework has a structural gap.

This guide covers the six essential ESG clause categories, sector-specific drafting considerations, and the enforcement landscape that makes these provisions commercially necessary.

---

## Why ESG Clauses Are Now Contractual Imperatives

The shift from voluntary ESG reporting to mandatory contractual compliance has been driven by three converging forces:

**Regulatory Mandates:** SEBI's BRSR framework now covers the top 1,000 listed companies, with BRSR Core assurance requirements for the top 250. These entities must report on ESG metrics across their value chain --- which means collecting verified data from suppliers, vendors, and partners.

**Supply Chain Accountability:** When a listed company reports on Scope 3 emissions, water consumption in its supply chain, or labor practices of its vendors, those data points must come from somewhere. Contracts are the mechanism for creating these reporting obligations.

**Enforcement Consequences:** Non-compliance triggers SEBI enforcement, National Green Tribunal penalties, green bond covenant breaches, and ESG rating downgrades that affect capital access and borrowing costs.

> **The Cascade Effect:** A listed company's BRSR obligation does not stop at its own operations. It flows down through every commercial relationship --- vendor agreements, procurement contracts, joint venture deeds, franchise agreements, and licensing arrangements. If the contract does not create the obligation, the data cannot be collected, and the BRSR filing is incomplete.

## The Six Essential ESG Clause Categories

### 1. Environmental Compliance

Environmental clauses must go beyond generic commitments to "comply with applicable laws." Effective provisions reference specific Indian statutes:

- Environment Protection Act, 1986
- Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1974
- Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1981

Key drafting elements include mandatory notification within a specified timeframe of any environmental violation, investigation, or penalty. The clause should also address environmental permits and clearances, requiring the counterparty to maintain all necessary approvals and provide evidence upon request.

### 2. Labor Standards

Labor provisions address the social dimension of ESG. Essential requirements include:

- **Child labor prohibition** with explicit reference to the Child and Adolescent Labour (Prohibition and Regulation) Act
- **Wage compliance** including adherence to the Code on Wages, 2019, covering minimum wages, equal remuneration, and timely payment
- **Non-discrimination** provisions aligned with the Equal Remuneration Act and constitutional protections
- **Occupational safety** standards referencing the Occupational Safety, Health and Working Conditions Code, 2020

The clause should require periodic certification of compliance and grant audit rights to verify labor standards in the counterparty's operations.

### 3. Anti-Corruption

The Prevention of Corruption Act, 2018, introduced corporate criminal liability for organizations whose associated persons offer bribes. This makes anti-corruption clauses commercially critical, not just aspirational.

Effective provisions require:

- A commitment to comply with the Prevention of Corruption Act and any applicable foreign anti-bribery laws
- Prohibition on facilitation payments
- Requirement to maintain anti-corruption policies and training programs
- Reporting obligations for any allegations, investigations, or proceedings
- Right to terminate for breach without liability

### 4. Supply Chain Sustainability

This is where BRSR compliance directly intersects with contract drafting. Suppliers must agree to:

- Report Scope 1 and Scope 2 greenhouse gas emissions on a defined schedule (quarterly or annually)
- Disclose water consumption and wastewater treatment practices
- Report waste generation, including hazardous waste disposal methods
- Flow down equivalent obligations to their own sub-contractors and sub-suppliers

The reporting schedule and format should align with the BRSR Core framework to minimize friction in data aggregation.

### 5. Carbon Disclosure

Carbon-specific clauses address:

- Verified GHG emissions reporting aligned with GHG Protocol standards (Scope 1, 2, and where applicable, Scope 3)
- Emission reduction targets and progress reporting
- Carbon credit procurement obligations where applicable
- Transition planning disclosures for carbon-intensive industries

For companies with science-based targets or Net Zero commitments, these clauses ensure that contractual partners are aligned with the company's decarbonization trajectory.

### 6. Waste Management

Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) obligations must be clearly allocated in contracts, particularly under:

- Plastic Waste Management Rules, 2016 (as amended)
- E-Waste (Management) Rules, 2016 (as amended)
- Hazardous and Other Wastes (Management and Transboundary Movement) Rules, 2016

The clause should specify which party bears EPR registration and compliance obligations, how costs are allocated, and what documentation must be maintained.

## Consequences of Non-Compliance

The enforcement landscape is multi-layered:

**SEBI Actions:** Non-compliance with BRSR filing requirements can trigger adjudication proceedings, penalties, and enhanced scrutiny of the listed entity.

**National Green Tribunal:** Environmental violations by a supply chain partner can result in NGT proceedings against the contracting company, particularly where the company had knowledge or should have had knowledge of the violation.

**Financing Implications:** Green bond covenants and sustainability-linked loan terms typically include ESG compliance representations. A breach by a material counterparty can trigger covenant defaults.

**Rating Impact:** ESG rating agencies (CRISIL, MSCI, Sustainalytics) assess supply chain ESG management. Contracts without ESG provisions signal weak governance and can lead to rating downgrades.

## Sector-Specific Drafting Considerations

### Manufacturing

- Emission intensity thresholds per unit of production
- Hazardous substance restrictions (referencing REACH-equivalent Indian standards)
- Circular economy obligations: recycled content requirements, end-of-life take-back provisions
- Water intensity targets and zero liquid discharge commitments

### Information Technology

- E-waste management and responsible disposal obligations
- Data center energy efficiency standards (PUE targets)
- Digital accessibility requirements
- AI ethics provisions where applicable (bias testing, transparency obligations)

### Banking, Financial Services, and Insurance (BFSI)

- RBI ESG guidelines for lending and investment portfolios
- Climate risk assessment integration into credit evaluation
- Responsible lending safeguards
- Green finance framework alignment

### Real Estate

- Green building certification requirements (IGBC, GRIHA)
- RERA compliance intersection with ESG commitments
- Energy performance covenants for building operations
- Sustainable material sourcing requirements

## Drafting Best Practices

### Specificity Over Generality

A clause stating "the Supplier shall comply with all applicable environmental laws" is nearly unenforceable in practice. Instead, enumerate the specific statutes, specify the reporting obligations, define the metrics, and set the frequency.

### Graduated Remedies

Not every ESG breach should trigger termination. Structure your remedies in tiers:

- **Non-material breach:** Notice and cure period (30--60 days), with requirement to submit a corrective action plan
- **Material breach:** Right to suspend the relationship, withhold payments, and demand enhanced reporting
- **Persistent or egregious breach:** Termination for cause without liability

### Audit Rights

Include a right to audit the counterparty's ESG compliance, either through direct inspection or third-party verification. Specify notice requirements, frequency limitations, cost allocation, and treatment of audit findings.

### Data and Reporting

Define precisely what data must be provided, in what format, on what schedule, and to whom. Align reporting requirements with BRSR Core metrics to ensure the data is usable for the listed entity's own compliance filings.

### Survival

ESG reporting obligations and environmental indemnities should survive termination of the agreement for a reasonable period (typically 3--5 years), particularly for environmental liabilities that may not manifest immediately.

## M&A and Vendor Considerations

### Due Diligence

ESG due diligence should assess:

- Pending environmental proceedings or investigations
- Labor compliance history, including any inspections or violations
- BRSR readiness and existing ESG reporting infrastructure
- Governance exposure, including anti-corruption compliance

### Vendor Onboarding

Integrate ESG into the vendor qualification process:

- ESG questionnaires as part of the onboarding package
- Compliance certifications before contract execution
- Baseline reporting before the first reporting cycle
- Periodic reassessment (annual or biennial)

### Phase 1: Assessment (Weeks 1--4)

Review existing contract templates across all categories (vendor, procurement, JV, franchise, licensing). Identify gaps against the six ESG clause categories. Prioritize based on BRSR materiality and counterparty risk.

### Phase 2: Drafting (Weeks 5--8)

Develop standardized ESG clause libraries for each contract type. Create tiered versions based on counterparty size and risk profile. Align reporting requirements with BRSR Core metrics.

### Phase 3: Implementation (Weeks 9--16)

Roll out updated templates for new contracts. Negotiate amendments to existing material contracts. Train commercial and procurement teams on ESG clause requirements.

### Phase 4: Monitoring (Ongoing)

Establish reporting collection and verification processes. Conduct periodic audits of high-risk counterparties. Update clause libraries as regulations evolve.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Are ESG clauses actually enforceable under Indian law?

Yes. ESG clauses are enforceable under the Indian Contract Act, 1872, like any other contractual provision, provided they are drafted with sufficient specificity. Vague aspirational language is difficult to enforce, but specific obligations with defined metrics, reporting schedules, and remedies for breach are treated as binding commitments.

### Should ESG breach trigger immediate termination?

Not necessarily. Best practice is graduated remedies: notice and cure for minor breaches, suspension and enhanced monitoring for material breaches, and termination rights reserved for persistent or egregious violations. Immediate termination rights are appropriate for criminal violations (e.g., child labor, bribery).

### How do ESG obligations flow down to sub-contractors?

The primary contract should include a flow-down clause requiring the counterparty to impose equivalent ESG obligations on its sub-contractors and sub-suppliers. The clause should also grant the principal the right to audit the sub-contractor's compliance and require the counterparty to report on sub-contractor ESG performance.

### What is the relationship between BRSR and contractual ESG clauses?

BRSR creates the reporting obligation for listed entities. Contractual ESG clauses create the mechanism for collecting the data needed to fulfill that obligation. Without contractual provisions requiring value chain partners to report ESG metrics, the listed entity cannot complete its BRSR filing --- particularly the BRSR Core assurance requirements.

### How should ESG clauses differ by sector?

Materiality drives the emphasis. Manufacturing contracts should focus on emissions, waste, and hazardous materials. IT contracts should address e-waste and energy efficiency. BFSI contracts should cover climate risk and responsible lending. Real estate contracts should address green building and energy performance. The six essential categories apply universally, but the specificity and weight given to each category should reflect sectoral risk.

## Conclusion

ESG clauses in Indian commercial contracts have moved from nice-to-have to must-have. SEBI's BRSR framework, the Prevention of Corruption Act's corporate liability provisions, and the National Green Tribunal's expanding jurisdiction mean that ESG non-compliance by a contractual counterparty is now a direct legal and financial risk to your organization.

The most effective approach is systematic: identify the six essential clause categories, draft with specificity rather than aspiration, build graduated remedies, and align reporting requirements with your own compliance obligations. ESG compliance is a supply chain problem, and contracts are the tool for managing it.
