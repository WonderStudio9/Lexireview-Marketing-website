# New Labor Codes India: What Changes in Your Employment Contracts

> **Import this article using Medium's import tool (medium.com/p/import) with canonical URL: https://lexireview.in/blog/new-labor-codes-india-employment-contract-changes**

India just replaced 29 labor laws with 4 consolidated codes. If your employment contracts still reference the Industrial Disputes Act 1947, they are referencing dead law. Here is exactly what changes.

---

## The Four New Labor Codes

India's labor law overhaul consolidates 29 central statutes into four codes enacted between 2019-2020. As states implement these on a staggered basis, compliance urgency is increasing for every employer whose contracts remain drafted under the old framework.

### Code on Wages, 2019

Replaces the Payment of Wages Act (1936), Minimum Wages Act (1948), Payment of Bonus Act (1965), and Equal Remuneration Act (1976). The headline change: a universal wage definition requiring basic salary to constitute at least 50% of total compensation.

### Industrial Relations Code, 2020

Replaces the Industrial Disputes Act (1947), Trade Unions Act (1926), and Industrial Employment Standing Orders Act (1946). Key changes include raising the retrenchment threshold from 100 to 300 workers and establishing fixed-term employment as a statutory category.

### Code on Social Security, 2020

Replaces nine laws including the Employees' Provident Funds Act (1952) and Payment of Gratuity Act (1972). Extends coverage to gig and platform workers and recalibrates benefits calculations based on the new wage definition.

### Occupational Safety, Health and Working Conditions Code, 2020

Replaces 13 laws including the Factories Act (1948). Redefines working hours, overtime calculations, annual leave entitlements, and contract labor obligations.

## Five Critical Changes to Employment Contracts

### 1. The 50% Wage Rule

This is the single most impactful change. Basic salary plus dearness allowance must equal a minimum of 50% of total remuneration. Any allowances exceeding 50% automatically recharacterize as wages for PF, gratuity, and bonus calculations.

**What this means financially** for a Rs.12,00,000 CTC employee:

| Component | Old Structure (30% Basic) | New Structure (50% Basic) |
|---|---|---|
| Basic Salary | Rs.3,60,000 | Rs.6,00,000 |
| Employer PF (12%) | Rs.43,200 | Rs.72,000 |
| Annual PF Increase | — | Rs.28,800 |
| Gratuity per 10-yr Employee | — | Increases by Rs.1,15,385 |
| Leave Encashment Increase | — | Rs.65,754 |

For a 500-employee organization, the incremental annual PF cost alone runs approximately Rs.1.44 crore.

> **Key Point:** The 50% rule applies to all employees regardless of sector or salary level. Most organizations with allowance-heavy compensation structures will see significantly increased statutory contributions.

### 2. Fixed-Term Employment Protections

Fixed-term employment is now a statutory category with specific protections:

- **Equal treatment** — fixed-term employees receive the same wages, hours, and benefits as comparable permanent employees
- **Pro-rata gratuity** — available even for contracts under five years (a significant departure from the old five-year minimum)
- **Termination distinction** — natural contract expiry does not trigger retrenchment compensation, but premature termination does

Your fixed-term employment agreements must explicitly include equal treatment provisions and pro-rata gratuity calculations.

### 3. Retrenchment Framework Changes

The retrenchment permission threshold has been raised from 100 to 300 workers. This means:

- Establishments with fewer than 300 workers no longer need government permission for retrenchment
- Retrenchment compensation is calculated at 15 days' average pay per completed year of service — but now calculated on the new (higher) wage definition
- Termination clauses must distinguish between different termination types and ensure procedural compliance

### 4. Gig and Platform Worker Provisions

For the first time, Indian legislation recognizes gig and platform workers and brings them within the social security framework. This creates significant recharacterization risk for businesses using independent contractors who are:

- Economically dependent on a single organization
- Working under organizational direction and control
- Using organizational tools and platforms

Employment contracts and independent contractor agreements need clear classification language and structural protections against recharacterization.

### 5. Working Hours and Overtime

The new framework establishes:

- **Daily cap:** 8 hours (12-hour shifts permitted in certain sectors with government approval)
- **Overtime rate:** Twice the ordinary wage rate, calculated on the new wage definition
- **Annual leave:** 1 day per 20 days worked
- **Quarterly overtime caps** varying by state rules

The higher wage base means overtime costs increase substantially for sectors like manufacturing, IT services, hospitality, and healthcare.

## 15-Point Compliance Checklist

### Compensation and Benefits

1. Restructure salary ensuring the 50% basic threshold is met
2. Update PF contribution clauses reflecting the new wage base
3. Revise gratuity calculations and add pro-rata gratuity for fixed-term workers
4. Ensure bonus eligibility aligns with Code on Wages provisions
5. Recalculate leave encashment on the new wage base

### Employment Type and Duration

6. Clearly classify all workers: permanent, fixed-term, contract, gig, or platform
7. Include equal treatment provisions in every fixed-term contract
8. Align probation clauses with standing order requirements

### Termination and Separation

9. Distinguish termination types and ensure retrenchment procedure compliance
10. Align notice periods with code requirements
11. Update severance calculations to 15 days' new wage per completed year
12. Review restrictive covenants against collective agreement conflicts

### Working Conditions

13. Cap daily hours at 8 (or 12 with government notification) and ensure 2x overtime wages
14. Align annual leave accrual with the 1-day-per-20-days structure
15. Reference OSH Code employer safety duties explicitly

## Frequently Asked Questions

### When do the new codes take effect?

Implementation is happening state by state with no single pan-India go-live date. Critical mass is expected by late 2026 or early 2027. Organizations should not wait for notification — preparing now avoids compressed timelines later.

### Does the 50% wage rule apply to all employees?

Yes. The rule applies regardless of sector or salary level. Organizations with allowance-heavy structures will see the largest financial impact.

### Can I use amendment letters for existing employees?

Yes. Amendment letters or addenda are acceptable for updating existing employment contracts, provided they comprehensively address all new code requirements. Document employee acceptance.

### Do fixed-term employees really get pro-rata gratuity?

Yes. This is a significant departure from the old regime where gratuity required five years of continuous service. Under the new codes, even short-term fixed contracts create gratuity obligations on a proportional basis.

### What about multi-state operations?

A single compliant national structure is recommended as the baseline, with state-specific adjustments only where genuinely necessary. This reduces administrative complexity while maintaining compliance.
