## The Legal Foundation: Indian Contract Act 1872

Before downloading any template, understand what makes an NDA legally enforceable in India. NDAs must satisfy the essential requirements of the Indian Contract Act: free consent, lawful consideration, lawful object, and competent parties.

The critical trap is Section 27, which voids agreements in restraint of trade. An NDA with overbroad restrictions on what the receiving party can do with general knowledge or skills acquired during a business relationship risks being struck down entirely. This is where most generic templates fail — they cast the net too wide.

Additionally, stamp duty payment is essential for enforceability. An unstamped NDA risks being inadmissible as evidence in court, though non-payment does not void the agreement itself. Stamp duty rates vary by state, making this a frequently overlooked compliance point.

## Three Types of NDAs

Before choosing a template, identify which type suits your situation:

**1. Unilateral NDA** — One party discloses confidential information to the other. Common in employee hiring and vendor engagements where information flows primarily in one direction.

**2. Mutual NDA** — Both parties exchange confidential information. Standard for partnerships, M&A discussions, joint ventures, and any relationship involving two-way information sharing.

**3. Multilateral NDA** — Three or more parties are involved. Used in consortiums, joint ventures with multiple partners, and complex multi-party transactions.

## 10-Clause NDA Template Structure

A properly drafted Indian NDA should contain these essential elements:

### 1. Definitions and Exclusions

Define "Confidential Information" with specificity. Equally important, define what is excluded: information already in the public domain, independently developed information, information received from third parties without restriction, and information required to be disclosed by law or court order.

> **Critical Point:** Overbroad definitions are the number one reason NDAs fail enforcement challenges in Indian courts. Be specific about what you are protecting.

### 2. Receiving Party Obligations

Spell out the standard of care — typically "reasonable efforts" equivalent to how the receiving party protects its own confidential information. Include prohibitions on copying, reverse engineering, and unauthorized disclosure.

### 3. Permitted Disclosures

Define narrow exceptions: employees and advisors with a need to know (who must be bound by similar obligations), disclosures required by law, and disclosures to legal counsel.

### 4. DPDP Act 2023 Compliance

This is the clause most pre-2023 templates lack entirely. If the confidential information includes personal data, the NDA must address Data Fiduciary and Processor roles, breach notification protocols, cross-border transfer restrictions, and deletion requirements upon termination.

### 5. Term and Survival

Specify the disclosure period (during which confidential information may be shared) and the survival period (during which obligations continue after the agreement ends). For trade secrets, consider indefinite survival. For general business information, 2-5 years is standard in India.

### 6. Remedies and Injunctive Relief

State explicitly that monetary damages may be inadequate and that the disclosing party is entitled to seek injunctive relief. This is critical because Indian courts are more likely to grant interim injunctions when the agreement specifically contemplates this remedy.

### 7. Return and Destruction of Information

Require the receiving party to return or destroy all confidential information upon termination or request. Include certification requirements for destruction and address the practical reality of backup systems and archived communications.

### 8. Non-Solicitation (If Applicable)

If needed, include carefully scoped non-solicitation provisions. Remember Section 27 — these must be reasonable in scope and duration to survive judicial scrutiny.

### 9. Governing Law and Jurisdiction

Specify which state's laws govern (this affects stamp duty requirements and procedural rules) and designate exclusive jurisdiction. For cross-border NDAs, consider including an arbitration clause.

### 10. Execution Formalities

Proper execution on stamp paper of appropriate value, signatures by authorized representatives, and witness attestation where required by state practice.

## Seven Common Enforceability Failures

After reviewing thousands of Indian NDAs, these are the mistakes that render agreements unenforceable:

1. **Overbroad confidential information definitions** — trying to protect everything protects nothing
2. **Unreasonable perpetual durations** — courts view these skeptically for non-trade-secret information
3. **Section 27 violations** — NDA functioning as a disguised non-compete
4. **Missing exclusions clause** — no carve-outs for publicly available information
5. **Inadequate remedies specification** — not mentioning injunctive relief
6. **No governing law designation** — creates jurisdictional ambiguity
7. **Stamp duty non-payment** — renders the document inadmissible as evidence

## Context-Specific Guidance

### Investor NDAs

Investors often refuse to sign NDAs before seeing a pitch deck. When they do sign, keep the scope narrow: protect specific financial projections, proprietary technology details, and customer data. Avoid restricting the investor from evaluating competing opportunities.

### Employee NDAs

Integrate with the employment agreement rather than creating a standalone document. Address what happens to confidential information after termination, and ensure the NDA does not function as an unenforceable non-compete under Section 27.

### Vendor NDAs

Focus on data handling obligations, especially under the DPDP Act 2023. Include audit rights, sub-contractor restrictions, and clear data deletion timelines. Vendor NDAs should complement (not replace) a proper Data Processing Agreement.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is an NDA enforceable in India?

Yes, provided it meets Indian Contract Act requirements: free consent, lawful consideration, competent parties, and lawful object. The agreement must not function as an unreasonable restraint of trade under Section 27.

### What stamp duty do I need?

Stamp duty varies by state. Maharashtra, Delhi, Karnataka, and other states each have different rates and requirements. Non-payment makes the NDA inadmissible as evidence, though the agreement itself remains valid.

### How long should an NDA last?

The disclosure period typically matches the business relationship. Survival of confidentiality obligations depends on the nature of the information: 2-3 years for general business information, 5 years for sensitive commercial data, and indefinite for trade secrets.

### What happens if an ex-employee breaches an NDA?

You can pursue injunctive relief to prevent further disclosure and claim damages for losses caused by the breach. The NDA must have been properly executed and stamped to be enforceable.

### Does DPDP Act change NDA requirements?

Significantly. Any NDA involving personal data must now address data fiduciary obligations, breach notification, cross-border transfer provisions, and deletion requirements. NDAs drafted before 2023 almost certainly lack these provisions.
