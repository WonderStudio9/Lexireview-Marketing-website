# Employment Agreement Format India 2026: Compliant Template

India's employment law landscape changed dramatically with the new labor codes. If you are still using a 2020-era template, your contracts likely miss mandatory provisions on gratuity, the new definition of "wages," and revised termination notice requirements.

The gap between outdated templates and current compliance requirements is widening. This guide covers the essential clauses every Indian employment agreement needs in 2026, the statutory framework driving these requirements, and the practical drafting considerations that separate enforceable agreements from liability-creating ones.

---

## Understanding the Three Document Types

Before diving into clauses, it is important to distinguish between three documents that are often confused:

- **Offer Letter:** A pre-contractual proposal communicated after selection but before joining. It outlines key terms but is not the binding agreement itself.
- **Appointment Letter:** A statutory confirmation of employment required by many state Shops and Establishments Acts. It serves as formal proof of the employment relationship.
- **Employment Agreement:** The comprehensive, legally binding contract governing the entire employment relationship --- obligations, restrictions, benefits, and termination mechanics.

Most organizations need all three, and they serve different legal functions.

---

## The Legal Framework in 2026

Employment agreements must now comply with a layered statutory framework:

- **Indian Contract Act, 1872:** Foundational enforceability requirements --- free consent, lawful consideration, competence of parties
- **Code on Wages, 2019:** Restructures how compensation must be calculated, with significant implications for salary breakdowns
- **Industrial Relations Code, 2020:** Governs termination, fixed-term employment, and standing orders
- **Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023:** Imposes new obligations on employers as data fiduciaries processing employee personal data
- **State Shops and Establishments Acts:** State-specific requirements for working hours, leave, and registration

## The 13 Essential Clauses

### 1. Party Identification and Effective Date

Clearly identify both the employer entity (including CIN/registration details) and the employee. Specify the date the agreement becomes effective, which may differ from the joining date.

### 2. Role, Responsibilities, and Reporting Structure

Define the designation, department, reporting manager, and primary responsibilities. Include a provision allowing reasonable modification of duties within the scope of the role --- without this, reassignment can become contentious.

### 3. Compensation: The 50% Basic Salary Rule

> **Critical Change:** Under the Code on Wages, 2019, basic salary must effectively be at least 50% of gross salary. Allowances and other components cannot exceed 50% of total remuneration.

This restructuring impacts employer costs significantly because PF, gratuity, and overtime calculations are all based on the "wages" definition. Many organizations still use pre-2019 structures where basic salary was 30--40% of gross. These structures are now non-compliant and create liability exposure.

Detail the full compensation structure: basic salary, HRA, special allowances, variable pay, and the basis for each calculation.

### 4. Probation Period

Specify the probation duration (typically 3--6 months), performance evaluation criteria, extension conditions, notice period during probation (often shorter than post-confirmation), and the process for confirmation or separation.

### 5. Working Hours and Leave

Reference the applicable state Shops and Establishments Act for working hour limits. Specify leave entitlements: earned leave, casual leave, sick leave, and any additional categories. Address overtime compensation where applicable under the Code on Wages.

### 6. Intellectual Property Assignment

All IP created during employment --- inventions, code, designs, content --- should vest with the employer through an explicit assignment clause. This is especially critical for technology companies. The clause should cover work created during business hours, using company resources, or related to the employer's business.

### 7. Confidentiality Obligations

Define confidential information broadly but specifically. Include obligations during employment and for a reasonable period post-termination (typically 2--3 years). Unlike non-compete clauses, confidentiality obligations that survive termination are enforceable in India.

### 8. Non-Compete: During Employment Only

> **Critical Legal Position:** Post-termination non-compete clauses are void and unenforceable in India under Section 27 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872.

This is one of the most common mistakes in Indian employment agreements. US or UK templates routinely include post-employment non-competes that Indian courts will not enforce. During-employment non-compete provisions are valid, but anything restricting the employee's right to practice their profession after leaving is void.

### 9. Non-Solicitation

Unlike non-compete, narrowly scoped non-solicitation clauses (prohibiting active solicitation of clients or employees for 12--24 months post-termination) are generally enforceable. The key is narrow scope --- a blanket prohibition on any contact with former colleagues is unlikely to survive judicial scrutiny.

### 10. Termination Mechanics

Address all scenarios: termination by employer with notice, termination by employee with notice, termination for cause (with defined grounds), and immediate termination for gross misconduct. Specify notice periods for each scenario and whether garden leave applies.

Under the Industrial Relations Code, 2020, termination of workers in establishments with 300+ employees requires government approval. Ensure your clause aligns with this threshold.

### 11. DPDP Act Compliance

The Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023, requires employers to disclose:

- Categories of personal data processed (attendance, payroll, health records, biometrics)
- Purposes of processing
- Data retention periods
- Employee rights: access, correction, and erasure
- Cross-border data transfer practices
- Breach notification procedures

This is a new mandatory inclusion that most pre-2023 templates lack entirely.

### 12. Statutory Benefits

Explicitly reference entitlements under PF, ESI, and gratuity. Under the revised framework, gratuity calculations use the new "wages" definition, which increases the payout. Include a statement that statutory benefits are provided in accordance with applicable law as amended from time to time.

### 13. Governing Law and Dispute Resolution

Specify the governing law (state jurisdiction matters for Shops and Establishments Act compliance) and dispute resolution mechanism. Many organizations prefer arbitration for senior employees, but note that certain employment disputes may fall outside arbitrable subject matter.

## State-Specific Considerations

Employment law compliance varies by state. Key differences include:

| Aspect | Maharashtra | Karnataka | Delhi | Tamil Nadu |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Registration | Mandatory S&E registration | Mandatory S&E registration | Mandatory S&E registration | Mandatory S&E registration |
| Working hours | 9 hrs/day, 48 hrs/week | 9 hrs/day, 48 hrs/week | 9 hrs/day, 48 hrs/week | 8 hrs/day, 48 hrs/week |
| Earned leave | 1 day per 20 days worked | 1 day per 20 days worked | 1 day per 20 days worked | 1 day per 20 days worked |
| Notice period | As per contract | 1 month typical | As per contract | As per contract |

For multi-state employers, the agreement should reference "the applicable Shops and Establishments Act" rather than naming a specific state law.

## Startup-Specific Provisions

Startups face additional drafting requirements:

**ESOP Vesting and Termination Treatment:** Clearly state the vesting schedule, cliff period, accelerated vesting triggers (if any), treatment of unvested options upon termination (forfeiture vs. partial vesting), and exercise window post-departure.

**Prior Invention Schedule:** Include an exhibit where the employee lists any pre-existing IP they bring to the role. This prevents disputes about whether an invention was created before or during employment.

**Moonlighting Policy:** Rather than a blanket prohibition, consider an approval-based mechanism where employees can pursue external work with written permission. This is increasingly expected in the Indian tech sector.

## Eight Common Drafting Mistakes

1. **Using US/UK templates without adaptation** --- Non-compete clauses, at-will employment provisions, and other foreign-law concepts do not work in India
2. **Omitting IP assignment clauses** --- Without express assignment, IP ownership becomes contentious, especially for technology companies
3. **Ignoring the DPDP Act** --- Post-2023, employment agreements must address data protection obligations
4. **Relying on unenforceable non-compete provisions** --- Section 27 makes post-termination non-competes void
5. **Not restructuring salary under the Code on Wages** --- The 50% basic salary requirement changes employer costs
6. **Using a single template for all states** --- Shops and Establishments Act requirements vary
7. **Failing to update for fixed-term employment** --- The Industrial Relations Code introduces specific provisions for fixed-term workers
8. **Omitting dispute resolution mechanisms** --- Relying on default court jurisdiction when arbitration may be more efficient

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is a written employment agreement legally required in India?

While the Indian Contract Act does not mandate a written contract, various state Shops and Establishments Acts require written appointment letters. Beyond legal requirements, a written agreement is essential for enforcing confidentiality, IP assignment, and non-solicitation provisions.

### Can I include a post-termination non-compete clause?

No. Post-termination non-compete clauses are void under Section 27 of the Indian Contract Act. Instead, protect your interests through confidentiality obligations, IP assignment, and narrowly scoped non-solicitation provisions.

### How does the 50% basic salary rule affect existing employees?

The Code on Wages, 2019, requires that basic wages constitute at least 50% of gross remuneration. Organizations need to restructure salary breakdowns, which increases PF and gratuity contributions. Existing employees should receive revised salary structures reflecting the new definition.

### What DPDP Act provisions must be included?

At minimum: categories of data processed, processing purposes, retention periods, employee rights (access, correction, erasure), cross-border transfer disclosures, and breach notification obligations.

### Are electronic employment agreements valid?

Yes. Under the Information Technology Act, 2000, electronically signed agreements are valid and enforceable. Digital signatures under Section 3 of the IT Act have the same legal effect as handwritten signatures.

## Conclusion

Indian employment agreements in 2026 must navigate a significantly more complex landscape than even five years ago. The Code on Wages, the Industrial Relations Code, and the DPDP Act have each introduced requirements that most legacy templates do not address. Getting these wrong does not just create HR headaches --- it creates compliance liability, unenforceable restrictions, and potential penalties.

The most effective approach is to start from the statutory requirements, layer in commercial protections that Indian courts will actually enforce, and build in flexibility for multi-state compliance. Templates should be living documents, updated as regulations evolve.
